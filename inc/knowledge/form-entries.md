# Viewing Form Entries

When someone submits a form on your site, their details are saved in WordPress so you can refer back to them at any time — even if the email notification got missed or went to spam.

---

## Finding Your Entries

1. Go to **Fluent Forms** in the left-hand admin menu.
2. You'll see a list of all forms on the site.
3. Click **Entries** next to the form you want to check.

This opens a table of every submission that form has received.

---

## Reading an Entry

Click on any row to open the full submission and see every field the person filled in — name, email, message, and anything else the form asks for.

Use the **Previous** and **Next** buttons at the top to move between entries without going back to the list.

---

## Filtering & Searching

At the top of the entries list you can:

- **Search** by name, email, or any text from the submission
- **Filter by date** to narrow down a specific period
- **Filter by status** — all entries, read, unread, or starred

Entries are marked **unread** until you open them, so the unread filter is useful for catching anything you haven't seen yet.

---

## Starring Entries

Click the star icon on any entry to mark it for follow-up. Use the **Starred** filter to pull them back up later.

---

## Exporting Entries

If you need to share entries or work with them in a spreadsheet:

1. From the entries list, click **Export Entries** (top right).
2. Choose **CSV** for Excel or Google Sheets, or **Excel** for a `.xlsx` file.
3. The file will download to your computer.

You can export all entries or apply filters first to export only a specific date range or status.

---

## Deleting an Entry

To remove a single entry, open it and click **Delete** — or from the list view, hover over the row and click **Delete**.

Deleted entries cannot be recovered, so only remove something if you're sure you no longer need it.

> Deleting an entry from WordPress does not unsend any email notifications that were already delivered.
