# Editing Pages

Pages on this site are built with **Bricks Builder**, a visual editor that lets you edit content directly on the page — what you see is what you get. You won't use the standard WordPress text editor for pages.

---

## Opening a Page to Edit

1. Log in and go to **Pages** in the left-hand menu.
2. Find the page you want to edit.
3. Hover over the page title and click **Edit with Bricks**.

This opens the visual editor in a new tab where you can click directly on any element to edit it.

> If you accidentally click **Edit** instead of **Edit with Bricks**, you'll see a notice saying the page is managed with Bricks. Close that tab and use **Edit with Bricks** from the Pages list instead.

---

## Editing Text

1. Click on any text in the editor — it will get a blue outline to show it's selected.
2. **Double-click** to go into typing mode and make your changes.
3. Click somewhere else on the page when you're done.

---

## Swapping an Image

1. Click the image you want to change.
2. In the panel on the right, click the current image thumbnail.
3. The media library opens — select an existing image or upload a new one.
4. Click **Select** to apply it.

---

## Saving

Click **Save** in the top-right corner, or press `Ctrl + S` (Windows) / `Cmd + S` (Mac).

Changes go live straight away on any page that's already published — there's no separate publish step once a page is live.

---

## Things to Leave Alone

Unless your developer has specifically asked you to:

- Don't move, add, or delete **sections or containers** — these control the overall layout
- Don't change anything in the **Style** tab of an element — shared styles affect the whole site
- Don't touch **Global Settings** or **Theme Styles** from the top Bricks menu
