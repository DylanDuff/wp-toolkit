# Site Settings

Global settings — things like your logo, contact details, social media links, and the announcement bar — are all managed in one place called **Branding & Extras**.

You'll find it in the left-hand admin menu.

---

## Logo

1. Go to **Branding & Extras** in the admin menu.
2. Make sure you're on the **Branding** tab.
3. Click the current logo image (or the upload area if none is set).
4. Choose an existing image from the media library or upload a new one.
5. Click **Update Options** at the bottom to save.

---

## Contact Details

1. Go to **Branding & Extras → Contact Settings**.
2. Update any of the following as needed:
   - **Phone Number**
   - **Email Address**
   - **Primary Address**
3. Click **Update Options** to save.

### Operating Hours

Operating hours are managed as a list of rows — one per day or time block.

- To add a day: click **Add Row**, enter the day name and open/close times
- To remove a day: click **Remove** on that row
- To show something like "Closed" or "By Appointment" instead of times: leave the time fields blank and use the **Override Text** field for that row
- Click **Update Options** when done

---

## Social Media Links

1. Go to **Branding & Extras → Social Settings**.
2. Enter the full URL for any social profiles you want to display (e.g. `https://www.instagram.com/yourbusiness`).
3. Leave any unused platforms blank — they won't show on the site.
4. Click **Update Options** to save.

Supported platforms: Facebook, Instagram, X (Twitter), LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok, Pinterest, WhatsApp, Reddit, LinkTree, Vimeo, and Google Business Profile.

---

## Announcement Bar

The announcement bar displays a message at the top of every page on the site.

1. Go to **Branding & Extras → Announcement Bar**.
2. Toggle **Enabled** on to show the bar, or off to hide it.
3. Edit the content in the text area — you can use bold, links, and other basic formatting.
4. Click **Update Options** to save.

---

## Map Settings

If your site displays an embedded map, the location is controlled here.

1. Go to **Branding & Extras → Map Embed**.
2. Update the **Latitude** and **Longitude** values to move the pin.
3. Optionally upload a custom **Map Icon** to replace the default pin image.
4. Click **Update Options** to save.

> If you need to find your coordinates, search for your address in Google Maps, right-click the pin, and copy the numbers shown.

---

## What to Leave Alone

The following areas are best left to your developer:

- **Settings → Permalinks** — changing this can break links across the site
- **Appearance → Theme File Editor** and **Plugins → Plugin File Editor** — these edit live code
- **Bricks → Settings** — theme-level configuration best handled by your developer
- **Users** — adding or changing account roles and permissions
